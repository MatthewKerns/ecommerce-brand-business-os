# General E-Commerce Category - TikTok Content Templates

## Category Overview

**Target Audience**: Broad consumer base across demographics - bargain hunters, quality seekers, problem solvers, gift-buyers, lifestyle upgraders, and impulse purchasers

**Key Purchase Drivers**:
- Value for money (price vs. quality)
- Problem-solving effectiveness
- Social proof and validation
- Convenience and time-saving
- Lifestyle enhancement and self-improvement
- Trust and credibility signals
- Emotional connection and aspiration

**Content Strategy Focus**:
- Demonstrate clear value proposition
- Build trust through authentic social proof
- Address common objections head-on
- Create "save for later" moments (high utility content)
- Show product in real-world context
- Leverage FOMO and scarcity psychology
- Make complex decisions simple

**Conversion Goals**:
- High save rate (reference content, purchase consideration)
- Link clicks (direct purchases, comparison shopping)
- Shares (recommendations, value broadcasting)
- Comments (engagement, social proof building)

## Category-Specific Hook Templates

### Hook Template 1: The Value Reveal (Pattern Interrupt for Price Skeptics)

**Category**: Pattern Interrupt

**When to Use**:
- Premium-priced products with hidden value
- Products that seem expensive but deliver ROI
- Addressing price objections proactively
- Justifying higher price points

**Hook Template**:
```
"I thought $[price] was expensive for [product] until I [discovered/calculated/realized this]..."
```

**Visual**: Close-up of product, price tag visible or mentioned, then transition to value demonstration

**Examples**:
```
"I thought $89 was expensive for a water bottle until I calculated what I was spending on disposable ones..."

"This [product] costs [price] but here's why I'm actually saving money..."

"I thought $150 was crazy for [product]... then I used it for a month"

"Spent $[price] on this. Here's what it replaced..." [show pile of cheaper alternatives]

"Before you say it's too expensive, let me show you the math..."
```

**Why It Works**:
- **Objection handling** - Addresses price concern immediately
- **Curiosity gap** - They want to see the justification
- **Relatability** - Viewer had same price skepticism
- **Value framing** - Shifts focus from cost to benefit

**Variations**:
```
"Everyone says this is overpriced until they see what it actually does..."

"This costs more than competitors. Here's why I don't regret it..."

"$[price] seemed like a lot. Then I calculated the cost per use..."

"I judged this by its price. I was wrong. Here's why..."
```

**Next Beat** (What comes after the hook):
Show the calculation/comparison, demonstrate longevity or multi-functionality, reveal hidden benefits or cost savings.

### Hook Template 2: The Problem Amplification (Curiosity Gap for Pain Points)

**Category**: Curiosity Gap

**When to Use**:
- Products that solve subtle or unrecognized problems
- Problems the viewer didn't know they had
- Products with non-obvious benefits
- Creating awareness before presenting solution

**Hook Template**:
```
"If you [common behavior], you're making the same mistake I made for [time period]..."
```

**Visual**: Show the "wrong way" first, creating recognition and tension

**Examples**:
```

"I was doing [task] wrong for 5 years... and you probably are too"

"You're wasting money on [category] if you don't know this..."

"I wish someone told me this before I bought 3 wrong [products]..."

"Everyone does [task] this way. It's wrong. Here's why..."
```

**Why It Works**:
- **Pattern interrupt** - Suggests viewer is doing something wrong
- **Curiosity** - They want to know what the mistake is
- **Authority** - You've learned from experience
- **Urgency** - Implies ongoing waste/loss

**Variations**:
```
"I wasted $[amount] before I learned this one thing about [category]..."

"You don't need a new [product]. You need to know this..."

"Stop buying [common solution]. Here's what actually works..."

"Three years and $[amount] later, I finally figured out [category]..."
```

**Next Beat** (What comes after the hook):
Explain the mistake clearly, show the negative consequences, introduce your product as the better solution.


- **Relatability** - You had the same doubts

**Variations**:

## Universal E-Commerce Best Practices

### 1. Hook Selection Strategy

**Match Hook to Product Strength**:
- **Strong social proof** → Use Social Proof Cascade hook
- **Price objection expected** → Use Value Reveal hook
- **Solves clear problem** → Use Problem Amplification or Time Saver hook
- **Competitive category** → Use Comparison Simplifier hook
- **Premium positioning** → Use Value Reveal or Lifestyle Integration hook
- **New/unknown product** → Use Discovery Moment or Problem Amplification hook

### 2. Trust-Building Elements

Always include at least 2-3 of these credibility signals:
- **Time-tested**: "I've been using this for [X months]..."
- **Personal investment**: "I bought this with my own money..."
- **Social proof**: "[X] people bought after my last video..."
- **Comparison**: "I tested this vs. [competitors]..."
- **Specific results**: "This saved me [specific number/time]..."
- **Transparent downsides**: "It's not perfect - [minor con] - but..."

### 3. Visual Best Practices

**First 3 Seconds**:
- Product must be visible and identifiable
- Good lighting (natural light or ring light)
- Stable shot (tripod or stable surface)
- Clean background or contextual setting

**Throughout Video**:
- Show product in use, not just talking about it
- Close-ups of key features (fill the frame)
- Multiple angles if demonstrating functionality
- Text overlay for key stats or price (readable font, high contrast)

**Final Frame**:
- Product clearly visible
- Link sticker positioned prominently
- Clean, uncluttered final shot

### 4. Conversion Optimization

**High Save Rate Tactics** (for consideration phase):
- Include specific numbers, stats, or comparisons viewers will want to reference
- Create "gift guide" or "best options for X" content
- Provide decision-making frameworks (comparison criteria)
- Share educational insights beyond just the product

**High Link Click Tactics** (for purchase phase):
- Create urgency: "Sells out fast," "Limited stock," "Sale ends [date]"
- Clear value proposition: "Best [category] under $[price]"
- Strong call-to-action: "Link in bio to grab yours"
- Specific next step: "Check reviews before you buy - link in bio"

**High Share Tactics** (for reach):
- Surprising reveals or test results
- Money-saving tips or price comparisons
- Gift recommendations
- Problem-solving for common pain points

### 5. Platform-Specific Optimizations

**TikTok**:
- Keep videos 45-60 seconds (sweet spot for retention + watch time)
- Use trending sounds strategically (don't force it)
- On-screen text for key points (many watch without sound)
- Native TikTok editing (less polished often performs better)
- Post 1-3x per day for algorithm favor

**TikTok Shop Integration**:
- Link product directly in video
- Use "Yellow Dot" indicator for shoppable videos
- Include product showcase in comments
- Pin comment with discount code if applicable

### 6. Authenticity Markers

**Do's**:
- Use natural language ("honestly," "I'm not gonna lie," "here's the thing")
- Show minor imperfections or limitations
- Share real usage timeline ("I've had this for 3 months")
- Speak directly to camera (build connection)
- Show real environment, not staged set

**Don'ts**:
- Don't use overly scripted, formal language
- Don't make unrealistic claims
- Don't hide sponsored relationships (disclose properly)
- Don't oversell - enthusiasm is great, but stay credible
- Don't ignore obvious flaws

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Feature Dumping
❌ **Wrong**: "It has 12 features including [list]..."
✅ **Right**: "It does 3 things really well: [benefit], [benefit], [benefit]"

**Why**: Viewers don't care about features, they care about what it does for them.

### 2. Vague Value Propositions
❌ **Wrong**: "This is really good quality..."
✅ **Right**: "I've dropped this 20 times and it still looks brand new..."

**Why**: Specificity builds trust; vague claims create skepticism.

### 3. Weak CTAs
❌ **Wrong**: "Let me know if you have questions..."
✅ **Right**: "Link in bio if you want to stop wasting time on [problem]"

**Why**: Clear, benefit-driven CTAs drive action; passive CTAs get ignored.

### 4. Ignoring Objections
❌ **Wrong**: Only talking about positives
✅ **Right**: "Yes, it's not the cheapest, but here's why it's worth it..."

**Why**: Addressing objections builds trust and overcomes purchase barriers.

### 5. Poor Hook-Content Match
❌ **Wrong**: Hook promises comparison, video is just demo of one product
✅ **Right**: Hook promises comparison, video delivers side-by-side test results

**Why**: Disappointing viewers kills retention and trust.

### 6. No Credibility Signals
❌ **Wrong**: "This product is amazing, link in bio"
✅ **Right**: "I've tested this for 6 months, compared it to 3 competitors, here's why it won..."

**Why**: Without credibility, you're just another person trying to sell something.

## A/B Testing Strategies

### Test Variable 1: Hook Style

**Test Setup**: Create 2-3 versions of the same video with different hooks:

**Version A**: Value Reveal Hook
```
"I thought $79 was expensive until I calculated what this replaced..."
```

**Version B**: Social Proof Hook
```
"400 people bought this after my last video... here's why they're obsessed"
```

**Version C**: Problem Amplification Hook
```
"I wasted $200 on [alternatives] before I found this..."
```

**What to Measure**:
- First 3-second retention (which hook stops the scroll best)
- Overall completion rate (which hook leads to full video watch)
- Save rate (which hook creates most value perception)

**Expected Outcome**:
- Social proof hooks typically perform best for awareness/reach
- Value reveal hooks typically perform best for consideration/saves
- Problem hooks typically perform best for conversion/link clicks

---

### Test Variable 2: Video Length

**Test Setup**: Same content, different lengths:

**Version A**: 45 seconds (tight, fast-paced)
**Version B**: 60 seconds (more detail, slightly slower)
**Version C**: 30 seconds (ultra-concise, single focus)

**What to Measure**:
- Completion rate by length
- Save rate by length
- Link click rate by length

**Expected Outcome**:
- 45-60s typically best for conversion (enough time to build trust + demonstrate value)
- 30s best for reach (higher completion rate = better algorithm push)
- Test your specific audience preferences

---

### Test Variable 3: Credibility Signal Type

**Test Setup**: Same video structure, different credibility signals:

**Version A**: Time-based credibility
```
"I've been using this every day for 4 months..."
```

**Version B**: Comparison-based credibility
```
"I tested this against 5 competitors..."
```

**Version C**: Social proof credibility
```
"12 friends have bought this after seeing mine..."
```

**What to Measure**:
- Trust indicators in comments ("seems legit," "ok I'm convinced")
- Link click rate (which credibility type drives most conversions)
- Save rate (which creates most consideration)

**Expected Outcome**:
- Comparison credibility typically strongest for conversion
- Social proof typically strongest for reach/shares
- Time-based typically strongest for trust with existing audience

---

### Test Variable 4: CTA Style

**Test Setup**: Same video, different ending CTAs:

**Version A**: Direct Purchase CTA
```
"Link in bio to grab yours before they sell out"
```

**Version B**: Low-Pressure Research CTA
```
"Link in bio to check reviews and see if it's right for you"
```

**Version C**: Value Reminder CTA
```
"If you're tired of [problem], this is the solution - link in bio"
```

**What to Measure**:
- Link click rate by CTA type
- Comments indicating purchase intent
- Save vs. click ratio

**Expected Outcome**:
- Direct CTAs typically drive most immediate clicks
- Research CTAs typically drive higher save rate (consideration phase)
- Value Reminder CTAs typically drive most qualified clicks (better conversion rate)

## Advanced Techniques

### 1. The "Cost Per Use" Justification

**When to Use**: Premium-priced products, durability-focused items

**Technique**:
```
"Yes, it's $[price], but here's the math: I've used it [X] times in [timeframe]. That's [price/uses] per use. The cheap version breaks after [shorter timeframe] and costs $[lower price]. You'll buy it [X] times to match the lifespan of this one. $[lower price] x [X] = $[total]. This is actually cheaper."
```

**Example**:
```
"Yes, it's $150, but here's the math: I've used it 200 times in 6 months. That's 75 cents per use. The $30 version breaks after 30 uses. You'd buy it 7 times to match this. $30 x 7 = $210. This is actually $60 cheaper, plus less hassle."
```

### 2. The "Replacement Stack" Visual

**When to Use**: Products that replace multiple items

**Technique**:
Show physical pile or list of items your product replaces, calculate total cost of alternatives

**Example**:
```
"This replaced: [show each item] - $20, $35, $15, $40, $25 = $135 total. This does all of that for $89, takes up 1/5 the space, and actually works better."
```

### 3. The "Before I Knew This" Series

**When to Use**: Creating content series, building audience anticipation

**Technique**:
Create multi-part series showing the learning journey

**Part 1**: "Things I wish I knew before buying [product category]..."
**Part 2**: "I tested 5 [products] so you don't have to..."
**Part 3**: "After using the winner for 30 days, here's the honest review..."

### 4. The "For [Specific Person]" Targeting

**When to Use**: Gift-giving seasons, targeting decision-makers

**Technique**:
Create multiple versions of same video, each targeting different gift recipients

**Examples**:
- "For the person who's always cold..."
- "For the friend who has everything..."
- "For the parent who needs their life easier..."
- "For the person starting [new chapter]..."

### 5. The "Real World Stress Test"

**When to Use**: Products with durability claims, building trust through transparency

**Technique**:
Show product surviving real (harsh) use cases over time

**Example**:
```
"Day 1: Testing durability claim
Day 7: Dropped it 10 times
Day 30: Still using it daily
Day 90: Here's what it looks like now [show wear]
Verdict: [honest assessment]"
```
