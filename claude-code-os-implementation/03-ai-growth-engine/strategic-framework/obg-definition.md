# One Big Obsessional Goal (OBG)

## The Concept
Your OBG is the single overriding objective that everything else serves. It's the answer to: "If I could only accomplish ONE thing in the next 12 months, what would have the biggest impact?"

## OBG Alignment Framework

### Does It Serve the OBG?
For any task/project/opportunity, ask:

1. **Direct Impact** (0-100%)
   - Does this improve CTR, CVR, or CM3%?
   - Does this advance the new product pipeline?
   - Can you trace a clear line to profitability?

2. **Time Investment**
   - Is this the best use of time toward profitability?
   - Does this fall under Pillar 1 or Pillar 2?

3. **Resource Efficiency**
   - Does ROI justify the investment?
   - Roger Vi principle: "It is better to have no sale than negative margin sale with no ranking benefit"

### Scoring Guide
- **90-100%**: Critical path - Must do NOW (listing tests, price changes, validated R&D)
- **70-89%**: Important - Schedule this week (PPC refinement, supplier outreach)
- **50-69%**: Useful - Add to backlog (branding improvements, content refresh)
- **<50%**: Misaligned - Don't do (new features without validation, speculative opportunities)

---

## OBG Protection

### What to Say NO To
- New product ideas that aren't thoroughly validated
- Advertising spend that doesn't generate ranking benefit or profit
- Feature additions that don't improve CTR/CVR
- Opportunities outside the Amazon marketplace (for now)
- "Good" ideas that distract from the two pillars

### Kill List
Things explicitly rejected to protect OBG focus:
1. Launching products without full validation process
2. Running broad PPC campaigns with no ROI tracking
3. Price points in the "no man's land" (neither value leader nor premium)
4. Guesswork optimization - all changes must be tested
